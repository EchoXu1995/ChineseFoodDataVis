02 | No Religious Constraints

Historically, most Chinese supreme rulers employed ancient philosophies (mainly Confucianism) as their guidelines to rule the country instead of relying on a single religion. Although there have been religious taboos, such taboos have never resulted in mandatory legislation. Relatively free from taboos and avoidances, Chinese people dare to taste different foods.

03 | The Influence of Traditional Chinese Medicine

In the book, The Food of China, ANDERSON, E. N. says “Chinese traditional beliefs worked very well to keep people healthy and to keep the food production system diverse. Many plants and animals that would not otherwise have been domesticated or kept in domestication, were grown because of their alleged medical values. ”


Medicinal Value of Chicken Organs According to Chinese Medicine

Chinese people have a complicated and very ancient science of nutrition and medicine. The theory of Chinese medicine holds that almost all foods have medicinal value, and there is no clear boundary between food and medicine.


Therefore, the Chinese nation should not be labeled as "barbaric" or "greedy" simply because of these "bizzare" foods. Instead, these foods are the reason why Chinese civilization has survived for thousands of years. Thanks to the food culture, Chinese people“have achieved a unique balance with their world, a unique success at supporting maximum populations over maximum time.”


Chinese food is a part of Chinese culture, and Chinese people are feeling proud of it!
